Tennis

Tennis is a racket sport known for its strategic depth and passionate fan base. Players use rackets to hit a ball over a net, aiming to score points by landing the ball in the opponent's court. The game's tactical complexity, combined with the athleticism and skill required, makes it a captivating spectacle for both players and spectators.

WHAT IS TENNIS?

Tennis or lawn tennis is often regarded as one of the most physically demanding sports around. Tennis has thrown up various global stars in different eras - from Rod Laver and Martina Navratilova to Pete Sampras, Steffi Graf, Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, Novak Djokovic and Serena Williams. The four marquee tennis tournaments every year, the Grand Slams - Australian Open, French Open, Wimbledon and the US Open - provide high-quality action throughout the year in addition to various lower-level tournaments to keep fans engaged.

EQUIPMENT USED

Tennis ball: According to the International Tennis Federation (ITF), a typical tennis ball should weigh between 56-59.4 grams with a diameter of 6.54-6.86 centimeters. They should be yellow or white in colour, though most balls are yellow.
Tennis racket: A tennis racket cannot exceed 73.7 cm in length and 31.7 cm in width. The racket shall have a frame enclosing sturdy strings, usually made of nylon, interwoven in crossed and bonded pattern, and a handle.
The player is required to hit the ball with the middle of the racket - the part with strings which is also known as hitting surface.

TENNIS COURT

A tennis court is a rectangular playing surface used for the sport of tennis. It is divided by a net into two equal halves and features specific markings for singles and doubles play. The court can be made of various materials, such as grass, clay, hard surfaces, or artificial turf, each influencing the speed and style of play. Standard dimensions are 78 feet (23.77 meters) long and 27 feet (8.23 meters) wide for singles, and 36 feet (10.97 meters) wide for doubles. The surface and surroundings are designed to provide a consistent and fair playing environment for players.

HOW TO PLAY TENNIS?

In tennis, the match begins with a coin toss to decide who serves, receives, or picks a side. The server stands behind the baseline and must hit the ball diagonally into the opponent's service area. Each player gets two serves per point. A fault occurs if the serve hits the net or lands outside the service area, giving the server a second attempt. A foot fault, where the server's foot touches the baseline or sideline, also results in a second serve. A double fault (faulting both serves) gives the point to the opponent. If the serve hits the net but lands in the correct area, it's a "let," and the server gets another chance. An ace, where the serve is not returned, wins the point for the server.

HOW ARE POINTS SCORED?

In tennis, each point starts with a serve, and the receiver's goal is to return the ball. Players then engage in a rally, hitting the ball back and forth. A point is won if the ball is hit before it bounces twice or if the opponent fails to return it. Players must wait for the ball to cross the net and cannot step over it. After a game, server and receiver roles switch, and in doubles, the receiving team alternates players for each point.

SCORING SYSTEM

A player or team has to win four points to win a game. Any game starts at 0-0 and the zero point in tennis is called love. The progression of points occurs as 1st(15),2nd(30),3rd(40) and 4th(Game) However, if both players win three points each in a game (i.e score is 40-40), then it's called a deuce. After deuce, the player who wins the next point has advantage. If the player/team who has advantage wins the next point, then they win the game. However, if the opposing player wins the next point after advantage, then the score moves back to deuce. A player/team needs to win two consecutive points after deuce to win a game.

INDIANS IN TENNIS

Indian tennis has seen several notable players who have made significant contributions to the sport. Leander Paes, one of India's greatest tennis players, is renowned for his doubles success, including multiple Grand Slam titles. Mahesh Bhupathi, another doubles specialist, achieved Grand Slam victories and formed a successful partnership with Paes. Sania Mirza became India's most successful female player, winning multiple Grand Slam titles in doubles and mixed doubles. Rohan Bopanna and Sumit Nagal have also made their mark, with Bopanna winning a Grand Slam in mixed doubles and Nagal gaining recognition for his performances in singles.

MAJOR TENNIS TOURNAMENTS

The four major tennis tournaments, known as the Grand Slams, are the most prestigious events in the sport. They are:
1.Australian Open: Played on hard courts in Melbourne in January, it marks the first Grand Slam of the year.
2.French Open: Held on clay courts in Paris in May-June, it is known for its challenging surface and long rallies.
3.Wimbledon: Played on grass courts in London in June-July, it is the oldest and most traditional of the Grand Slams.
4.US Open: The final Grand Slam, played on hard courts in New York in August-September, is known for its fast-paced matches and vibrant atmosphere. Winning a Grand Slam is the pinnacle of success in tennis.
