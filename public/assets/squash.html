SQUASH

Squash is a fast-paced racquet sport played either one-on-one (singles) or with two teams of two players each (doubles). It’s played on a four-walled court, and the objective is to hit a small rubber ball against the front wall in such a way that the opponent cannot return it before it bounces twice.

WHAT IS SQUASH?

Squash is a fun and challenging contest of agility, strategy, talent, and mental and physical strength. It is an incredibly beneficial game for health and fitness, and has been voted the number one healthiest sport by Forbes Magazine. Along with its health benefits, squash has the ability to be played year-round, in all kinds of weather, and by players of any skill level – making it a sport for everyone to love!

EQUIPMENT USED

Racket: Squash racquets are lightweight, typically weighing between 110 to 160 grams, and have a small, oval-shaped head. They are designed to provide control and power while being easy to maneuver.
Ball:The squash ball is small, rubber, and comes in various types that differ in bounce and speed. The most common balls are color-coded (e.g., yellow for extra-duty, red for standard) to indicate their level of bounce and suitability for different court surfaces.

SQUASH COURT

A squash court is a four-walled space, measuring 9.75 meters long and 6.4 meters wide. It features a front wall with an "outline" at the top and a "tin" at the bottom. The side and back walls are also in play. The court includes service boxes where the server stands, and various lines that define play areas. Courts are often constructed from wood or glass, with glass walls popular in professional settings for viewing purposes. The floor is marked with a short line that divides the court into two halves. The front wall's upper boundary ensures the ball stays within the play area. The walls are typically painted white or have a reflective surface to improve visibility. The court's dimensions and markings are standardized to ensure consistent play across different locations.

HOW TO PLAY SQUASH?

To play squash, hit the ball against the front wall so your opponent can’t return it before it bounces twice. Serve from one of the service boxes, making sure the ball hits above the service line and lands in the opposite box. During rallies, the ball must hit the front wall before bouncing on the floor. Points are scored when your opponent fails to return the ball or hits it out of bounds. The game requires quick reflexes and strategic positioning.

HOW ARE POINTS SCORED?

In squash, points are scored based on several key factors. You earn a point if your opponent fails to return the ball before it bounces twice on the floor. The ball must be hit before it bounces twice, so if your opponent misses or lets it bounce twice, you win the point. Additionally, points are awarded if the opponent's shot goes out of bounds; the ball must strike the front wall between the top boundary (outline) and the bottom boundary (tin). If the ball hits above the outline or below the tin, it’s out of play, and you score a point.

HOW TO WIN IN SQUASH?

To win in squash, score more points than your opponent by successfully hitting the ball against the front wall so they cannot return it before it bounces twice. Points are earned when your opponent fails to make a valid return, hits the ball out of bounds, or obstructs your play. Matches are typically played to 11 or 9 points, and the player who first reaches the required number of points wins the game. Matches are often played as the best of three or five games.

INDIANS IN SQUASH

Indians have made significant strides in squash with players like Saurav Ghosal, Dipika Pallikal Karthik, and Joshna Chinappa achieving international success. Ghosal has been a top-ranked player, Pallikal has won multiple PSA titles, and Chinappa has also made notable contributions. These players have helped raise the profile of squash in India and increased its popularity. Their achievements have inspired a new generation of players and fostered greater interest and support for the sport across the country.

MAJOR SQUASH TOURNAMENTS

1.PSA World Championships: The premier event on the professional squash circuit, featuring the world's top players competing for the title of world champion.
2.British Open: Known as the "Wimbledon of Squash," this historic tournament is one of the sport's most prestigious events.
3. World Team Squash Championships: A team event where countries compete for the world title, held every two years.
4.Commonwealth Games: Squash is featured in this multi-sport event, with individual and team competitions representing Commonwealth nations.
