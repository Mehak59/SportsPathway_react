TABLE TENNIS

Table tennis, also known as ping pong, is a popular indoor sport that involves hitting a small ball back and forth across a net-covered table using small paddles. It is a fast-paced game that requires quick reflexes, hand-eye coordination, and strategic thinking. Players can compete individually or in doubles, and the game is typically played to a set number of points or games. Table tennis is enjoyed by people of all ages and skill levels, and it is a popular sport in many countries around the world.

WHAT IS TABLE TENNIS?

Table tennis, also known as ping pong, is a fast-paced indoor sport played on a rectangular table divided by a net. The objective is for players to hit a lightweight ball back and forth across the table using small paddles, aiming to make the ball land on the opponent's side in such a way that they cannot return it.

EQUIPMENT USED

Table: The primary playing surface, a rectangular table divided into two halves by a net. It is typically made of wood or a similar sturdy material.
Ball: A small, lightweight ball made of celluloid. It is slightly larger than a ping pong ball and has a slightly softer bounce.
Paddles: Flat, wooden or composite paddles with a rubber surface. The rubber surface provides grip and control when hitting the ball.
Net:A fine mesh net stretched across the middle of the table, dividing the playing area into two halves.
Racket case: A case used to store and transport paddles and balls. It helps protect the equipment from damage.

TABLE TENNIS COURT

The playing surface in table tennis is called a table. It is rectangular in shape, divided into two halves by a net. The dimensions of a standard table tennis table are 274 cm (108 in) long, 152.5 cm (60 in) wide, and 76 cm (30 in) high. The net is 15.25 cm (6 in) high at the edges and 18.19 cm (7.16 in) high in the center.

HOW TO PLAY TABLE TENNIS?

Table tennis matches typically begin with a coin toss to determine who serves first. The player who wins the toss can choose to serve or receive. Once the server is determined, the match begins with a serve, where the server tosses the ball upward and hits it over the net. The opponent must return the ball back over the net before it bounces a second time on their side. The game continues with players alternating serves after each point, and the first player to reach the predetermined number of points wins the game. The match is typically played to a set number of games, with the player who wins the most games emerging victorious.

HOW ARE POINTS SCORED?

Points in table tennis are scored based on the outcome of each rally. A rally is a series of strokes between the players until the ball is no longer playable. Points are awarded to the player who successfully returns the ball to the opponent's side, or if the opponent fails to return the ball, hits the ball out of bounds, or touches the net during play (except for the serve). The first player to reach the predetermined number of points wins the game, typically 11 points with a 2-point advantage required.

SCORING SYSTEM

Scoring in table tennis is based on the outcome of each rally. A rally is a series of strokes between the players until the ball is no longer playable. Points are awarded to the player who successfully returns the ball to the opponent's side, or if the opponent fails to return the ball, hits the ball out of bounds, or touches the net during play (except for the serve). Typically, a game is played to 11 points with a 2-point advantage required to win. If the score reaches 10-10, the game continues until one player has a 2-point lead. The first player to win the best-of-seven games wins the match.

INDIANS IN TABLE TENNIS

Indians have made significant contributions to the sport of table tennis. There have been several Indian players who have achieved international success and represented the country at major tournaments. One of the most notable Indian table tennis players is Sharath Kamal Achanta. He has been a dominant force in Indian table tennis for many years and has won numerous medals at international competitions, including the Commonwealth Games and Asian Games.

MAJOR TABLE TENNIS TOURNAMENTS

Here are some of the most prestigious table tennis tournaments worldwide:
World Table Tennis Championships: The highest level of international competition in table tennis, held biennially.
Olympic Games: Table tennis is a popular event at the Olympic Games, with both singles and doubles competitions.
ITTF World Cup: A prestigious annual tournament featuring the top-ranked players in the world.
ITTF World Tour: A series of international tournaments held throughout the year, offering valuable ranking points to players.
