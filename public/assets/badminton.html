BADMINTON

Badminton is a fast-paced racket sport known for its agility, speed, and strategic play. Players use rackets to hit a shuttlecock over a net, aiming to score points by landing it in the opponent's court. The sport's quick reflexes, precise control, and tactical depth create an exciting and dynamic experience for both players and spectators.

WHAT IS BADMINTON?

Badminton is one of the most popular sports in the world, with a passionate fan following around the globe. It is also one of the biggest draws at multi-sport spectacles like the Olympics. A regular fixture since the Barcelona 1992 Olympics, badminton now has five disciplines at the Games after mixed doubles was introduced at Atlanta 1996. While badminton is most popular in Asia, it also attracts great interest in Europe with players from Denmark among those regularly challenging for top honours. Want to learn more about badminton? Here's a look at the rules and equipment you need to play.

EQUIPMENT USED

Racket: A lightweight racket made of materials like carbon fiber or aluminum, designed for speed and control.
Shuttlecock:It has a rounded cork base, which is often covered with thin leather, and is topped with a skirt made of 16 overlapping feathers. The unique design allows it to be aerodynamically stable, ensuring it flies smoothly through the air while rapidly decelerating. Shuttlecocks are lightweight, usually weighing between 4.74 to 5.50 grams.

BADMINTON COURT

Badminton Courts are the rectangular surfaces used for the racket sport of badminton. Courts are usually marked for both singles or doubles games with boundary widths varying between the two match types. Badminton courts are surfaced with safe flooring materials for gameplay. Badminton Courts have a length of 44’ (13.4 m), but double courts are 20’ (6.1 m) wide while single courts are reduced to 17’ (5.18 m); shrinking by 1.5’ (.46 m) on both sides. Service courts are split by a center line dividing the width of the court and are set back from the net by a ‘short service line’ of 6.5’. Doubles games also require a ‘long service line. Clearances of 2’ (.61 m) should be providing around the entire badminton court.

HOW TO PLAY BADMINTON?

In badminton, players use rackets to hit a shuttlecock over a net, aiming to land it in the opponent’s court. The game starts with a serve, and players take turns hitting the shuttlecock, trying to score points by making it bounce within the opponent’s court boundaries. Points are won if the shuttlecock lands in the court, if the opponent fails to return it, or if they commit a fault. The game requires quick reflexes, precise shots, and strategic play to outmaneuver the opponent.

HOW ARE POINTS SCORED?

Points in badminton are scored using the rally scoring system, where a point is awarded on every serve, regardless of which player served. A player earns a point by landing the shuttlecock in the opponent's court, if the opponent fails to return it, or if they commit a fault. Matches are typically played to 21 points, with the winner needing at least a 2-point lead. If the score reaches 20-20, the game continues until one player gains a 2-point advantage.

SERVE IN BADMINTON

The birdie must be hit below waist height, with players serving diagonally into their opponent’s service box. Both players must remain stationary until the serve is made. As per badminton singles rules, the server starts from the right service court, and will serve from that side every time they have an even amount of points. A player serves from the left every time they have an odd amount of points. Each player will retain serve for as long as they keep winning points. If the receiving side takes the point, they assume serve. Going forward, the player who did not initially serve for each team will only assume the service once their side has won a point as the receiving side.

INDIANS IN BADMINTON

Indian badminton has gained prominence with several notable players. P.V. Sindhu is a standout, winning multiple international titles, including Olympic medals. Saina Nehwal has also achieved significant success with her numerous titles and Olympic medal. Kidambi Srikanth and Lakshya Sen have made their mark in men’s badminton, with Srikanth winning several international tournaments and Sen earning accolades at global events. These players have greatly contributed to the sport's growth and popularity in India.

MAJOR BADMINTON TOURNAMENTS

1.The Thomas Cup: It is the World Men’s Team Badminton Championships. The first competition was held in 1948. Now it takes place every two years in even-numbered years. Three singles and two doubles are played in the game.
2.Uber Cup: World Women’s Team Badminton Championships are held every two years in odd-numbered years since 1956, consisting of three singles games and two doubles games.
3.The World Badminton Championships: It takes place every two years in odd-numbered years. It consists of singles and doubles matches between men and women.
4.The Sudirman Cup: The final Grand Slam, played on hard courts in New York in August-September, is known for its fast-paced matches and vibrant atmosphere.
